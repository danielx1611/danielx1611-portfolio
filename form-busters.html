Form Busters

Project Overview: Boggle Solver

The current PDF-based form system at FIT has been widely criticized by both students and advisors since its introduction in 2018. The lack of a real-time tracking system forces students to rely on email communication, making the process slow and inefficient. Our web application, Form Buster, aims to modernize this system by enabling students to track the status of their forms, while administrators can efficiently approve, modify, and manage submissions in one centralized platform. This solution enhances the user experience for students while significantly improving administrative workflow and efficiency.

Approach:

  • Web-Based Form Tracking for Students

    Students can log in, select a form, and fill in the required details. Profile information (e.g., name, student ID) is auto-populated for convenience. Once submitted, students can track the form’s progress in real time, with a structured list of approvers based on the form type.

  • Creation & Management of Registrar Forms

    Administrators (registrar staff) can create new forms using a built-in form builder, offering complete control over form fields and the approval workflow. Existing forms can be modified, saved as drafts, published, or retired when no longer needed.

  • Automated Email Notifications

    The system automatically notifies students when they are eligible to submit specific forms (e.g., graduation petitions, CLEP exam eligibility). Additionally, students receive updates when their forms are modified, accepted, denied, or finalized. Advisors and staff receive reminders when they need to review or sign a pending form.

Key Features & Functionalities:

  • Three user roles: Students, Faculty Advisors, and Admins

  • Students can submit course registration forms digitally, eliminating the need for email-based PDF submissions.

  • Students can track the approval status of their forms, a feature currently unavailable.

  • Administrators can create, update, and modify forms online, replacing the manual PDF editing and distribution process.

Technology Stack & Implementation:

The application is built using the MERN Stack, leveraging:

  • MongoDB for database management

  • Express.js and Node.js for backend functionality

  • React.js for a dynamic frontend interface

  • HTML & CSS for styling and UI/UX design

By integrating these technologies, Form Buster provides a seamless and efficient alternative to FIT’s outdated PDF-based system, streamlining form submissions for students and administrators alike.
